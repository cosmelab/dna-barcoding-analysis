Understanding Quality Control

What are Phred Quality Scores?

Phred quality scores indicate the confidence in each base call. Q20 means 99% accuracy (1 in 100 chance of error), Q30 means 99.9% accuracy (1 in 1000 chance of error).

Why do some sequences fail?

  • Too short: Sequences under 500bp may not provide enough information for species identification
  • Low quality: Poor chromatogram peaks can lead to incorrect base calls
  • Contamination: Mixed signals from multiple DNA sources
  • Degraded DNA: Old or improperly stored samples

How to improve sequence quality

  1. Ensure DNA is fresh and properly stored (-20¬∞C or -80¬∞C)
  2. Use high-quality primers designed for your target region
  3. Optimize PCR conditions (annealing temperature, cycle number)
  4. Clean PCR products before sequencing (remove primers and dNTPs)
  5. Request re-sequencing for failed samples

What does the chromatogram show?

The chromatogram displays raw fluorescence signals from the sequencer. Clean, sharp peaks with good separation indicate high-quality sequence data. Overlapping peaks or noisy baselines suggest quality issues.
